Introduction

Motivation

The goal of our project is to build a classifier that can identify whether a news article is fake or real, based on the text and title content of each article. We believe this task is important because misinformation and fake news plays a huge role in influencing social media interactions and democratic processes, as seen by the controversy surrounding the 2016 presidential election. Being able to detect if a news article is ‘real’ or not would be extremely useful to many social media platforms, who could use these machine learning methods to suppress viral fake news articles circulating on their websites, and thereby limit the flow of misinformation online.


Dataset

We found a dataset online containing 11,000 articles - specifically, the article’s title, text and a classification denoting whether the article is ‘real’ or ‘fake’. An article is defined as ‘real’ is one that has been written in a factual manner, and is consistent with ethical journalism practices; an article is defined as ‘fake’ if it is not ‘real’, including but not limited to satire, opinion pieces, and conspiracies.
